
**Avogadro's Law: Equal volumes of gases at the same temperature & pressure
contain equal numbers of molecules**

**Avogadro's Constant $N_{A}$ is the number of atoms in 12g of $C^{12}_{6}$ == $6.02\times {10}^{23}$ atoms**

molar mass is defined as the mass of one mole of the substance 
$\therefore$  
Mass = number of moles (n) x Molar Mass (M)

The molar mass of any substance is the nucleon number (top or bigger number) in grams
e.g. $C^{12}_{6}$ has molar mass of 12g $mol^{-1}$ 

---
When a gas molecule collides with the sides of the container, it's momentum changes from +u -> -u, therefore $\Delta p$ = 2u


**IDEAL GAS DEFINITION THIS IS IMPORTANT**
- The gas contains a very large number of atoms/molecules moving in random directions and random speeds
- The atoms themselves occupy negligible volume compared to the volume of the gas
- The collisions between the atoms with the walls and with each other are elastic
- The time of collisions is negligible compared to the time between collisions
- Electrostatic forces between atoms are negligible except during collisions
