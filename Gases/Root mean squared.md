In a gas, the average velocity is 0 $ms^{-1}$ due to all of the random directions, therefore we use a different measure of motion called the **Root mean squared (r.m.s.) speed**

1. The velocity of each molecule, c, is squared for all N molecules
   $c_{1}^{2},c_{2}^{2}, c_{3}^{2} \dots c_{N}^{2}$
2. The mean is then taken $\bar{c^{2}} = \frac{c_{1}^{2} + c_{2}^{2} \dots c_{N}^{2}}{N}$
3. Finally take the square root $c_{rms} = \sqrt{ \bar{c^{2}} }$
