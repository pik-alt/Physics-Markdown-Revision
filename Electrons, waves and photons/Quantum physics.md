**The photon model states that electromagnetic energy only exists in certain little packets (quanta)**

**A photon is a discrete packet of electromagnetic energy**

**The photon energy is proportional to the frequency**

$E=hf$ (f.b.)
$f  = \text{frequency}$
$h = \text{ Plank's constant} = 6.63\times {10}^{-34}$ (f.b.)

Photon's travel at the speed of light, so
$c=f\lambda \therefore E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$ (f.b.)


so shorter wavelength means higher photon energy

## The photoelectric effect

**Photoelectric effect: where a mobile electron absorbs a high frequency photon, giving it enough energy to escape the surface of the metal**

You can make a current flow through this, shining a lamp at a circuit with a gap and the electrons will jump the gap.

You also need to know how to use a gold leaf electroscope:

![[Gold leaf electroscope.png|478]]




#### Photoelectric effect characteristics:
1. Photoelectrons are normal electrons with mass $m_{e}$ and charge $e$
2. Photoelectrons are only released by electromagnetic waves above a minimum *threshold frequency $f_{0}$*, normally UV light
3. $f_{0}$ depends on the type of metal
4. Above $f_{0}$ the number of photoelectrons released per second is proportional to the intensity of light
5. The kinetic energy of the electrons is increased by increasing the frequency, not the intensity
6. Photoelectrons are released instantly when exposed to light with $f > f_{0}$


**The photoelectric effect cannot be explained by the wave theory of light**

The wave theory would suggest that you can shine a low energy light on metal and, since the wave is continuous, eventually the surface electrons would gain enough energy to escape

**The electron-photon interaction is one-to-one, they can't accumulate energy from more photons**

**Any remaining energy that isn't required to release the photoelectron goes to the photoelectron's kinetic energy**


## Einstein's photoelectric effect equation


Photon energy in = energy to release the electron + kinetic energy of the electron
(as energy must be conserved)

$hf = \phi + KE_{\text{max}}$ (f.b.)

$\phi$ is the work function of the metal, the minimum energy required for electrons to escape


![[EK against F.png|533]]


On a graph of maximum kinetic energy against frequency of light, the Y intercept is the work function $\phi$, the X intercept is the threshold frequency $f_{0}$, gotten from $EK_{\text{max}} = hf-\phi$
The gradient is Plank's constant $h$



### Stopping potential

Electrons that escape can be pulled back if positive voltage is applied to their plate.

$W = eV$

$eV = EK, \text{ the electron stops and the current is zero}$

**The stopping potential $V_{s}$ is the minimum potential difference which is required to reduce the maximum kinetic energy of a photoelectron to zero**

at zero current:
$hf = \phi + eV_{s} \therefore \phi = hf - eV_{s}$


Devices called photocells use the photoelectric effect to measure the intensity of light


## Wave particle duality

**Wave particle duality states that matter behaves like a wave when travelling through space and as a particle when interacting with other matter**


|        | Wave-like nature          | Particle-like nature                          |
| ------ | ------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Light  | Diffraction, interference | Photoelectric effect<br>Electron excitation   |
| Matter | Electron diffraction      | Newton's laws and<br>interparticle collisions |


**De Broglie wavelength: particles with momentum p have the de Broglie wavelength, $\lambda$ given by:**

$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$ (f.b.)


If you shoot electrons through graphite, the interatomic spacing of the graphite particles act like slits which the electrons diffract through, producing an interference pattern
