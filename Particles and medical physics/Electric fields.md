You always use low mass positive test charges for E-fields

The electric field strength, E, at a point in the field is defined as the force per unit charge on a positive test charge placed at that point.

$E=\frac{F}{q}$ (f.b.)

(must be a small test charge)

E field decreases with distance, as line density decreases

---
## Coulomb's law

$F = \frac{Qq}{4\pi \epsilon_0 r^2}$ (f.b.)


combining this and the first E-field formula, we get:

$E = \frac{Q}{4 \pi \epsilon_{0} r^2}$ (f.b.)

$\varepsilon_{0} =8.85\times {10}^{-12}$ (f.b.)

Permittivity of free space is a constant, and different materials can make it easier or harder for E-field lines to pass through

$\epsilon_{total} = \epsilon_{r} \epsilon_{0}$ 


---
## Electric potential

pretty much exactly the same as [[Gravitational Fields#Gravitational Potential|gravitational potential]]

$V=\frac{Q}{4\pi\varepsilon_{0}r}$ (f.b.)
V is the work done per unit charge to bring a positive test charge from infinity to that point


You can also get Electric Potential energy with this, which is the total work done:

$E_{V} = \frac{Qq}{4\pi\varepsilon_{0}r}$ (f.b.)


---
## Capacitance & E-Fields

Field lines in a [[Capacitors|capacitor]] are uniform everywhere except the edges, where they have a curve to them.

![[Capactior field fliens.png]]

$W=Fd$ 
work done moving a test charge q a distance d from positive to negative

$F=Eq$ 

combining:

$W=Eqd$ 

we can combine this with $V=\frac{W}{Q}$ to get:

$E=\frac{V}{d}$ (f.b.)


**Dielectric: an insulating material that increases the capacitance due to its molecules becoming polarised in an E-field**
see: [[Capacitors]]


**Field lines are non-uniform around curved conductors**


#### Millikan's oil drop experiment
Millikan levitated charged droplets between two oppositely charged plates and, at a certain electric field, the weight of the drop was balanced by the electromagnetic force.


$F=mg, F=eQ$

Using their density and volume, he found the mass and found that the charge of the droplets was always a multiple of the elementary charge $e$

$e=1.6\times {10}^{-19}$(f.b.)

You can do normal SUVAT shit with acceleration in E-fields just as in gravity, just remembering:

$F=Eq$ & $E=\frac{V}{d}$ & $F=ma$
