
Celsius scale (C): water freezes at 0$^{\circ}C$  and boils / steam point of 100$^{\circ}C$

Kelvin scale (K): based on the lowest possible temperature of 0K and the triple point of water 273.16K

One Celsius degree is the same size as one degree Kelvin

$\text{Temperature in K} = \text{Temperature in C} + 273$


at 0K, all motion of particles ceases, it is also impossible to reach

**If one object is hotter than another, net thermal energy (heat) Q (Joules) will flow from the hotter object to the colder one until they come into thermal equilibrium**

total heat out of hotter object = total heat into the colder object

---
## Internal Energy

**Internal energy, U, of an object is the sub of the kinetic and potential energies of all its atoms or molecules**

Gasses have the largest potential energy and solids have the smallest for some reason

As you heat a body, you increase either the kinetic or potential energy of the particles, meaning that it increases it's internal energy

## Specific heat capacity

SHC is the amount of heat required per unit mass to change its temperature by 1 degree

$c = \frac{Q}{m \cdot \Delta t}$
or
$E=mc\Delta t$
