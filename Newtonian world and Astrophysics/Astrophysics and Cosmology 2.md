## Astronomical distances

 We use these ways of measuring distance cause space is so damn big
 - $\text{Astronomical Unit (AU)} = 1.5\times {10}^{11}m$
 - $\text{Light Year (ly)} = 9.4\times {10}^{15}m$ (f.b.)
 - $\text{Parsec (pc)} = 3.1\times {10}^{16}m$ (f.b.)
 
 **The cosmological principle is the assumption that when viewed on a large enough scale, the Universe is homogeneous, and the laws of physics are universal**

There is no preference for direction, it appears the same everywhere, so there is no centre.

**One Astronomical Unit (AU) is defined as the mean distance between the Earth and the Sun, 1 AU = $1.50\times {10}^{11}m$**

**One light year (ly) is defined as the distance that light travels in the span of a year in a vacuum
1 ly = $9.4\times {10}^{15}m = 63,000\text{AU}$**

**Parallax is the apparent change in position of an object from a change in the position of the observer**

when you change your angle, things further away from you appear to move more than things closer to you.

**$1\degree = 60\text{ arcminutes} (60')$
$1' = 60 \text{ arcseconds (60'')}$

therefore:
$1\degree = 3600''$
 You can do this on your calc!

**The parsec (pc) is the distance at which a radius of 1 AU subtends an angle of one arcsecond (1/3600 of a degree)**

![[Parsec drawing.jpg]]


$\tan(1'') = \frac{1AU}{1pc}$
$\therefore$
$1pc = \frac{1AU}{\tan{1''}}$


because the angle we used is very small, we can use the tan small angle approximation where $\tan\theta \approx \theta$

so in general **$\theta = \frac{1}{d} \text{ arcseconds}$ (f.b.)
where d is the distance in parsecs**


The largest distance you can measure with this is around 100pc

## Doppler effect

The Doppler effect is what you experience when, for example, a car with a siren drives past you. The noise is higher pitched as it's driving towards you and lower pitched as it's driving away from you.

![[Doppler.png]]

This also works with light waves, though the speed has to be much less than $c$

The formula for this is:
$\frac{\Delta\lambda}{\lambda} \approx \frac{\Delta f}{f} \approx \frac{v}{c}$ (f.b.)

$\Delta\lambda = \lambda_{new} - \lambda_{original}$   and the same for frequency

towards the observer is counted as the positive direction

![[redshift.png]]

You can see that the spectra move more towards the lower frequency and so higher wavelength side (more towards red), so it's moving away, so it's been redshifted.

Longer wavelengths experience a larger shift, since the initial wavelength is higher, the change in wavelength must also be higher to keep the ratio constant
## Hubble's law

Hubble observed that:
**1. Most light coming from distant galaxies was redshifted, i.e. moving away from us**

**2. In general, the further away the galaxy, the higher the red shift and so the faster it was moving away from us**


**The recessional speed $v$ of a galaxy is *almost* directly proportional to its distance $d$ from Earth***

$v=H_{0}d$ (f.b.)



![[Hubble graph.png]]

Due to the large distances, Hubble plotted the graph of speed with $km s^{-1}$ and distance with $Mpc$ with the gradient being the Hubble constant

More modern measurements give Hubble's constant as $\approx 68Kms^{-1}Mpc^{-1}$
though this value has a lot of uncertainty and scatter in the results

Because the units are Kilometres per Mega parsec, which are both distances, they cancel out, leaving it as $H_{0} = 2.21\times {10}^{-18}s^{-1}$

This is interpreted as the rate of expansion of the Universe.


If we assume that all the galaxies started at the same point in the Universe (aka big bang theory), then after $t$ seconds, $t = \frac{d}{v}$, and since $v=H_{0}d$, $t=\frac{1}{H_{0}}$.

The reciprocal of Hubble's constant is the age of the Universe.

**Hubble's constant assumes that the Universe is expanding at a constant rate, the acceleration = 0**

also since the Hubble constant is related to the age of the Universe, which is always increasing (obviously), $H_{0}$ is always changing, so it's not really a "constant"


## The Big Bang Theory

**The Big Bang Theory states that the Universe started as a single point which exploded 13.8 billion years ago and space and time has been expanding and cooling ever since**

The two main pieces of evidence for this are:
1. **Hubble's Law: Red shift data shows that in general galaxies are getting further apart, interpreted as time and space expanding**

2. **Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB): The high energy short wavelength photons created in the big bang have been stretched over time to produce an ambient background radiation in the microwave region**


**CMB is the radiation that comes from all parts of the Universe with a peak wavelength in the microwave region, corresponding to a black body temperature of 2.7K**

**This is interpreted as the remnant radiation from all parts of the Universe from the hot big bang that has been red-shifted over 13.8 billion years as the Universe expanded**

since no other theory could account for this other than the Big Bang theory, this is why it was accepted


## Evolution of the Universe

Dark matter explains why galaxies spin faster than they should based only on visible matter calculations.
We estimate that around 84% of the Universe is dark matter.

Type 1a supernovas from white dwarfs absorbing nearby matter showed that the redshift and so recession speed of the supernova's was increasing with distance such that the expansion of the universe is accelerating.

No one has any idea what's going on, so physicists made up dark energy to explain this.
