A gravitational field is the force field around all objects of mass, which attracts other objects of mass within the field towards the centre of mass.

They extend to infinity, but get weaker as distance increases


Gravitational field strength, g, at a point is the force per unit mass on a small object placed at that point

#  g = $\frac{F}{m}$ 

The weight of an objects is the force that the gravitational field of the planet exerts on it

## Gravitational Potential

The gravitational potential $V_{g}$ at a point in a gravitational field is the work done per unit mass in moving an object from infinity to that point, scalar

@ $\infty$ , $V_{g} = 0$ 

consider the work done moving a mass m a distance r from the Earth, with mass M

$W = Fx \cos \theta$ 
$F = -\frac{GMm}{r^2}$  

$\theta = 180$
$W = \frac{GMm}{r}$

$V_{g}$ is work done per unit mass:

$V_{g} = \frac{GM}{r}$

## Gravitational Potential Energy

The GPE of an object mass m at a point in a gravitational field is the work done to move the mass from infinity to that point

$E = m \times V_{g}$ 

close to the surface:
$E = mgh$

### Escape Velocity

to escape the gravitational field:

$E_{k} = \Delta E_{g}$
